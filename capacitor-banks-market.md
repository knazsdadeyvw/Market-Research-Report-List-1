<p><p>Capacitor banks are essential components in power systems, classified into three main market types: Low Voltage (LV) and Medium Voltage (MV) capacitor banks, and High Voltage (HV) capacitor banks. LV and MV capacitor banks enhance power factor, stabilize voltage levels, and mitigate harmonics in industrial and commercial applications. Conversely, HV capacitor banks are utilized in transmission networks to support long-distance power delivery, improve system efficiency, and reduce transmission losses, catering primarily to utility-scale and large industrial operations.</p></p>
