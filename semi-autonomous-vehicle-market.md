<p><p>The semi-autonomous vehicle market is categorized into Level 2 and Level 3 vehicles. Level 2 vehicles, equipped with advanced driver-assistance systems, can control acceleration, braking, and steering simultaneously but require constant driver supervision. In contrast, Level 3 vehicles can handle all driving tasks in specific conditions, allowing the driver to disengage while the system operates. This distinction highlights the transition from partial to conditional autonomy, shaping consumer adoption and regulatory frameworks in the evolving automotive landscape.</p></p>
