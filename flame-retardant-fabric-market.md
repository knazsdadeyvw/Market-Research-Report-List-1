<p><p>The flame retardant fabric market is primarily divided into two types: inherent flame retardant fabric and treated flame retardant fabric. Inherent flame retardant fabrics possess built-in resistance to flames, as their fibers are naturally resistant to combustion, making them suitable for high-performance applications. In contrast, treated flame retardant fabrics are textiles that have undergone chemical treatment to enhance their fire resistance, offering versatility for various uses. Both types cater to industries such as aviation, automotive, and protective clothing, ensuring safety standards are met.</p></p>
